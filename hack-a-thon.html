2. Making Bioengineering Fun

Is there a work pipeline in your lab you have to do all the time? Do you always forget to push your changes to GitHub? Or you don’t have the time to adapt your protocol to the new summer research student in the lab? Perhaps you’re trying to understand a new concept and have no clue where to start looking. These are just a few examples where AI can free some of your schedule and help you out.

For this strand you will need to develop an AI powered solution to daily problems you might face in the lab (both wet and dry), or somewhere else --- the office, during literature review, at conferences, whenever and wherever you get a bright idea! Don’t worry about how useful your solution might be to others, you’ll be surprised how many people go through the same problems! Just get creative and have fun!

Potential ideas include:

  • Image-processing tool to count colonies from plates, quantify morphologies/sizes, and give a summary of the data.
  • ML algorithm to estimate the size of DNA / RNA fragments / proteins from gels and their relative concentrations.
